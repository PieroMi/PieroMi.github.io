About Via Margutta

“Fortuny, for example, came from Spain. Around 1858 he came to Rome to study Italian painting. It was an important school; this was the street of the painters.”
- Giovanni Battista Carboni

Via Margutta, once the home to the film director Fellini, also an attractive location for film directors to shoot scenes in Via Margutta. It is a short street but contains a lot of history dating back a couple thousand years. The word "Margutta" comes from a latin euphemism -"sea drop"- from the water drainage that came from the villas and palaces that surrounded what is now called Via Margutta. The street has always been known for artists and craftsman and not only locally but from many parts of Europe. Artists such as Claude Debussy, composer Giacomo Puccini, along with Pablo Picasso visited or lived there.

Filled with history and the impact that it made for the art world is what makes Via Margutta so valued and cherished. Furthermore, the correlation that Via Margutta has with the art gallery that we named after the street is because of what was mentioned in the introduction that while Luis Miranda was pursuing his academics in the School of Arts in Rome he won his first award and it was named the "Via Margutta" award in 1959. Because Via Margutta was known for art and its artists, Luis Miranda spent much of his time in that same street, having the opportunity to have seen Fellini and many other known people from the industry walking in the neighborhood.

As shown in the images above, the streets in the 1950s are filled with artists from all over showing and selling their art which have a lot of similarities to the image from Las Peñas, Guayaquil. The history of Las Peñas is that it is the first neighborhood in the city of Guayaquil. Many different personalities came across the neighborhood of Las Peñas from politics, to historians, artists, writers. Eleven Presidents of Ecuador lived in this neighborhood, writers such as Enrique Gil Gilbert, musician Antonio Neumane, the author of the lyrics of the National Anthem. Che Guevera lived in Las Peñas for sometime giving pediatric assistance to the inhabitants of the city. And other known people such as Ernest Hemingway, and Pablo Neruda visited the neighborhood.

In conclusion, in honor of Luis Miranda living his youth days as a student in Rome, winning his first award named Via Margutta, and spending much of his time in the street named Via Margutta it felt it was the right suited name to exhibit Luis Miranda's private collection in none other than Via Margutta Gallery and located in Las Peñas, Guayaquil where the artist also spent much of his teens exhibiting his art.
